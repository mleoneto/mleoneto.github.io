I am a PhD candidate in Marketing (Consumer Behavior) at New York University Stern School of Business. My research lies at the intersection of judgement and decision-making, persuasion, and moral and social psychology, with a focus on applying behavioral science to policy-relevant contexts. Much of my work examines how the framing of information—for instance, probabilistic, numerical forecasts or donation appeals—shapes judgment and choice.
